# Muscat 2040: Growth & Infrastructure — Executive Summary

**Prepared by:** Abdullah Al Junaibi | **Date:** March 2026 | **Challenge:** CodeStacker 2026 — Data Analytics

## Population Outlook

Muscat Governorate's population stood at **1.50 million** at end-2024 (NCSI). Using three growth scenarios calibrated against recent NCSI trends and Oman Vision 2040 drivers, we project:

| Scenario | Annual Rate | 2030 | 2040 | Growth |
|----------|------------|------|------|--------|
| **Low Growth** | 1.5% | 1.64M | **1.90M** | +27% |
| **Base Case** | 2.5% | 1.74M | **2.23M** | +48% |
| **High Growth** | 4.0% | 1.90M | **2.81M** | +87% |

**Key driver:** Expat labor demand — expatriates constitute 61% of Muscat's population. Economic diversification under Vision 2040 is the primary swing factor between scenarios.

## Infrastructure Risk: Healthcare 🏥

Muscat currently has an estimated **2,500 hospital beds** (1.67 per 1,000 people) — well below the WHO benchmark of 3.0 and the GCC average of 2.0.

| Scenario | Beds Needed (WHO 3.0) | Shortfall | Capacity Exceeded |
|----------|-----------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Low Growth | 5,708 | **2,808 beds** | Already |
| Base Case | 6,678 | **3,778 beds** | Already |
| High Growth | 8,425 | **5,525 beds** | Already |

**Critical finding:** Even with 400 planned beds (by 2028), Muscat's hospital bed ratio **already falls short of both WHO and GCC benchmarks today**. By 2040, the base case requires nearly **tripling** current capacity.

## Infrastructure Risk: Education 🎓

Muscat has approximately **330 schools** serving ~300,000 school-age students (20% of population). Current density is 900 students/school — well above the 600 student quality benchmark.

| Scenario | Students 2040 | New Schools Needed | New Teachers (15:1) |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Low Growth | 380K | +92 to +304 | +10,376 |
| Base Case | 445K | +164 to +412 | +14,685 |
| High Growth | 562K | +294 to +606 | +22,452 |

**Critical finding:** Under the base case, Muscat needs **~400 new schools** to reach quality benchmarks and **~15,000 additional teachers** by 2040.

---

## Breakpoint Years

Both sectors are **already under capacity strain** in 2024:

- **Healthcare:** Current beds/1,000 ratio (1.67) is below both WHO (3.0) and GCC (2.0) benchmarks
- **Education:** Current student density (900/school) exceeds the 600-student quality target

The gap **widens every year** without investment. Delay compounds the deficit.

---

## Three Recommended Actions

1. **Healthcare — Accelerate hospital construction.** The current pipeline of 400 beds is insufficient. Muscat needs an additional **2,500–5,000 beds** by 2040 depending on growth. Prioritize PPP models to attract private hospital investment.

2. **Education — Launch a school-building program tied to population data.** Plan for **10–25 new schools per year** through 2040, with priority in high-growth wilayats (Bawshar, Seeb). Simultaneously recruit and train 1,000+ teachers annually.

3. **Establish a dynamic infrastructure planning unit.** Link NCSI population data directly to infrastructure capacity dashboards. Use scenario-based planning (as modeled here) to trigger investment thresholds automatically — rather than reacting to shortages after they occur.
